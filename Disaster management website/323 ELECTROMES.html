Preventive Measures for Different Emergencies

Natural Disasters

Earthquake

What Should You Do Before Earthquake?

  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls to prevent tipping
  • Create an emergency plan and designate meeting points
  • Know how to shut off gas, water, and electricity

What Should You Do During Earthquake?

  • "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" during the shaking
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and exterior walls
  • If indoors, stay indoors; if outdoors, find an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines

How Can You Stay Safe After Earthquake?

  • Check for injuries and administer first aid
  • Beware of aftershocks and potential building damage
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates and evacuation orders

Hurricane

What Should You Do Before Hurricane?

  • Secure windows and doors with storm shutters or plywood
  • Trim trees and shrubs to reduce flying debris
  • Stock up on essentials, including water, non-perishable food, and medications

What Should You Do During Hurricane?

  • Stay inside and away from windows
  • If advised, evacuate to higher ground or a designated shelter
  • Follow local authorities' instructions and listen to weather updates

How Can You Stay Safe After Hurricane?

  • Wait for the all-clear from authorities before venturing outside
  • Avoid flooded areas and downed power lines
  • Check on neighbors and assist those in need if it is safe to do so

Tornado

What Should You Do Before Tornado?

  • Identify a safe room or storm shelter in your home
  • Have a battery-operated weather radio for alerts
  • Clear yard of loose objects that can become projectiles

What Should You Do During Tornado?

  • Seek shelter immediately in a basement or interior room without windows
  • Cover yourself with a mattress or heavy blankets to protect against flying debris
  • Do not try to outrun a tornado in a vehicle

How Can You Stay Safe After Tornado?

  • Watch out for hazards like broken glass and downed power lines
  • Use caution when entering damaged buildings
  • Report gas leaks or damaged utility lines to authorities

Wildfires

What Should You Do Before Wildfires?

  • Create a defensible space around your home by clearing flammable vegetation
  • Have an evacuation plan and emergency kit ready
  • Keep gutters, roofs, and decks clear of leaves and debris

What Should You Do During Wildfires?

  • Evacuate early if advised by authorities
  • Close all windows and doors to prevent embers from entering the house
  • Stay tuned to local news and weather updates

How Can You Stay Safe After Wildfires?

  • Wait for authorities to declare it safe before returning home
  • Check for hidden embers or hotspots around your property
  • Be cautious of ash, smoke, and damaged structures

Flood

What Should You Do Before Flood?

  • Know your area's flood risk and elevation
  • Elevate electrical systems and appliances above potential flood levels
  • Purchase flood insurance if you live in a high-risk area

What Should You Do During Flood?

  • Evacuate if necessary and move to higher ground
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters
  • Disconnect electrical appliances and turn off utilities if flooding is imminent

How Can You Stay Safe After Flood?

  • Avoid returning home until authorities declare it safe
  • Check for structural damage and potential hazards
  • Take photos of damages for insurance claims

Tsunami

What Should You Do Before Tsunami?

  • Familiarize yourself with tsunami evacuation routes
  • Have a designated meeting place for family members
  • Know the natural signs of an impending tsunami, such as the water receding rapidly

What Should You Do During Tsunami?

  • Move to higher ground immediately if you feel an earthquake or receive a tsunami warning
  • Stay tuned to emergency broadcasts for updates

How Can You Stay Safe After Tsunami?

  • Stay away from coastal areas until authorities declare them safe
  • Be cautious of potential aftershocks

Volcanic Eruption

What Should You Do Before Volcanic Eruption?

  • Be aware of the nearest evacuation routes and shelters
  • Have a mask to protect against ash inhalation
  • Keep a battery-operated radio for updates

What Should You Do During Volcanic Eruption?

  • Follow evacuation orders promptly
  • Stay indoors and keep windows and doors closed to prevent ash from entering
  • If caught outside, wear protective clothing and goggles

How Can You Stay Safe After Volcanic Eruption?

  • Check for injuries and administer first aid
  • Clean off ash carefully, as it can be abrasive and harmful if inhaled
  • Protect water sources from ash contamination

Heatwaves

What Should You Do Before Heatwaves?

  • Stay informed about heatwave warnings and forecasts
  • Stock up on water and electrolyte-rich beverages
  • Use fans or air conditioning to cool indoor spaces

What Should You Do During Heatwaves?

  • Stay indoors during peak heat hours (usually midday)
  • Avoid strenuous outdoor activities
  • Stay hydrated and wear lightweight, light-colored clothing

How Can You Stay Safe After Heatwaves?

  • Continue to stay hydrated and avoid exertion
  • Check on vulnerable individuals, such as the elderly and young children
  • Watch for signs of heat-related illnesses

Drought

What Should You Do Before Drought?

  • Conserve water by fixing leaks and using water-saving appliances
  • Plant drought-resistant vegetation
  • Have a plan for watering essential plants

What Should You Do During Drought?

  • Follow local water restrictions and conservation guidelines
  • Limit outdoor water usage
  • Be mindful of fire risks due to dry conditions

How Can You Stay Safe After Drought?

  • Continue water conservation efforts even after the drought ends
  • Replenish soil moisture through appropriate irrigation methods
  • Stay informed about the recovery of water sources

Manmade Disasters

Fire

What Should You Do Before Fire?

  • Install smoke detectors and fire extinguishers in key areas of your home or workplace
  • Develop a fire escape plan with multiple exit routes
  • Keep flammable materials away from heat sources

What Should You Do During Fire?

  • If a small fire breaks out and you're confident, use a fire extinguisher to put it out
  • If the fire is spreading or you can't control it, evacuate immediately
  • Crawl low under smoke to minimize inhalation

How Can You Stay Safe After Fire?

  • Do not re-enter the building until authorities confirm it is safe
  • Report any missing persons and provide relevant information to rescue teams
  • Cooperate with investigators to determine the cause of the fire

Industrial Accidents

What Should You Do Before Industrial Accidents?

  • Stay informed about industrial facilities in your area and their potential hazards
  • Understand the emergency protocols and evacuation plans at your workplace
  • Use personal protective equipment (PPE) as required

What Should You Do During Industrial Accidents?

  • Follow evacuation orders and emergency protocols immediately
  • Seek shelter in safe areas, if available, and stay away from the accident site
  • If exposed to hazardous substances, follow decontamination procedures

How Can You Stay Safe After Industrial Accidents?

  • Seek medical attention if you suspect exposure to hazardous materials
  • Follow instructions from authorities regarding re-entry to the affected area
  • Cooperate with investigations to understand the cause of the accident

Terrorist Attacks

What Should You Do Before Terrorist Attacks?

  • Stay informed about potential threats and emergency alerts in your area
  • Be vigilant and report suspicious activities to law enforcement
  • Know evacuation routes and safe meeting points

What Should You Do During Terrorist Attacks?

  • If there's an active shooter, run, hide, or fight as a last resort if trapped
  • In the event of a bomb threat, evacuate the area immediately
  • Follow the instructions of law enforcement and emergency responders

How Can You Stay Safe After Terrorist Attacks?

  • Stay away from the affected area until authorities declare it safe
  • Report any unattended bags or suspicious items to law enforcement
  • Offer support to victims and those affected by the attack

Cyber Attacks

What Should You Do Before Cyber Attacks?

  • Use strong, unique passwords for online accounts and enable two-factor authentication
  • Regularly back up important data and store it securely
  • Keep your computer's operating system and software up to date

What Should You Do During Cyber Attacks?

  • If you suspect a cyber attack, disconnect from the internet and other networks
  • Contact your IT department or a cybersecurity professional for assistance
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments

How Can You Stay Safe After Cyber Attacks?

  • Report the cyber attack to the appropriate authorities or your IT department
  • Change all passwords and review your accounts for unauthorized access
  • Take steps to strengthen your cybersecurity defenses

Chemical Spills

What Should You Do Before Chemical Spills?

  • Know the hazardous materials present in your area and their potential risks
  • Familiarize yourself with emergency response plans for chemical spills
  • Keep a supply of personal protective equipment (PPE) at home and work

What Should You Do During Chemical Spills?

  • Evacuate the area immediately if there's a chemical spill
  • If unable to evacuate, seek shelter in a safe and well-ventilated area
  • Avoid contact with spilled substances and contaminated areas

How Can You Stay Safe After Chemical Spills?

  • Follow decontamination procedures if exposed to hazardous chemicals
  • Stay away from the affected area until it's declared safe by authorities
  • Cooperate with cleanup and containment efforts

Building Collapse

What Should You Do Before Building Collapse?

  • Regularly inspect buildings for structural integrity and maintenance issues
  • Identify safe areas within buildings in case of collapse
  • Keep an emergency kit with essential supplies at home and work

What Should You Do During Building Collapse?

  • If you're inside a collapsing building, seek shelter under sturdy furniture or against an interior wall
  • Cover your head and neck to protect against falling debris
  • Use a whistle or shout to alert rescuers to your location

How Can You Stay Safe After Building Collapse?

  • Do not re-enter a collapsed building until it's deemed safe by authorities
  • Notify emergency services if you hear someone trapped in the rubble
  • Cooperate with rescue teams and provide any useful information

Transportation Accidents

What Should You Do Before Transportation Accidents?

  • Familiarize yourself with emergency procedures and exits when using public transport
  • Use seat belts and other safety devices when traveling by car or bus
  • Have emergency contact numbers saved on your phone

What Should You Do During Transportation Accidents?

  • Follow crew instructions and emergency procedures during a transportation incident
  • Stay calm and assist others if possible without putting yourself in danger
  • Use escape routes and emergency exits as directed

How Can You Stay Safe After Transportation Accidents?

  • Evacuate the area following the guidance of transportation staff or first responders
  • Seek medical attention if injured or if you feel unwell after an accident
  • Cooperate with investigators to provide information about the incident
